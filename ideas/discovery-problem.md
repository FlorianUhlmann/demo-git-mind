## The Problem

If you have done the work of distilling a conversation into a document, the next question begins immediately: Has someone already solved this? Are the problems you're working on already resolved somewhere you haven't looked?

Right now if you have an idea about a problem, finding the other people working on the same frontier requires enormous luck or institutional access. You find their conclusions maybe. You never find their reasoning. You never find the exact constraint they got stuck on that you might be able to solve. The connection that could have happened never does because the reasoning is private and inaccessible.

## The Lagrangian Metaphor

In fluid dynamics, Lagrangian coherent structures are the hidden skeleton of flow — the invisible boundaries that determine where particles end up. They aren't visible on the surface. But they govern everything beneath it.

Problems evolve like this:

```
conversation → idea → draft → argument → revision → new insight
```

Multiply that by every serious researcher, every curious person, every domain expert having these conversations every day. The collective reasoning potential being lost is staggering. Not because the model is bad. Because there's no vessel for it.

Imagine someone working on the same frontier finds your reasoning chain — not your paper, not your conclusion, but your *trajectory*. They find the exact moment in your reasoning where your path and theirs diverge. They can see what you tried. They can see where you got stuck. They can contribute precisely where they have something to add — not to the surface of your conclusion but to the interior of your reasoning chain.

## The Vision

What you're pointing at is a **web of reasoning**. Not documents linking to documents. Journeys linking to journeys. Where the cutting edge of any problem is not a paper or a blog post but a living reasoning chain — queryable, extensible, contributable — that anyone can plug into at the depth they're capable of.

This is what you mean by people meeting each other at the cutting edge. Not networking. Not citation. **Actual epistemic contact** — two reasoning chains finding each other at the exact point of productive friction.
