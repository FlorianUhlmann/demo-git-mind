The Problem

Three problems. No solutions.
Until now.

01

The Transition Tax

Your best thinking happens in conversation. It stays there. Moving it into a shareable format costs more than most people are willing to pay. The loop never closes.

02

The Dimensionality Problem

You spent three hours thinking through a hard problem. A collaborator gets the last paragraph. Documents are flat. You read them top to bottom. You cannot enter the thought.

03

The Merge Problem

Two people work on the same problem in parallel. Both make real progress. Then they try to combine it. You can merge text. You cannot merge reasoning.

The Solution

Think the document
into existence.

A closed loop between thinking and documenting. Every sentence anchored to the reasoning that created it.

⚓

Reasoning Anchor

Every sentence in a document links back to the exact chat moment that produced it. The document is a portal back into the thought.

◈

Knowledge Graph

Emerges as a byproduct. Every insight and question becomes a queryable node. The chat becomes an interface to everything the project has ever known.

⑂

Reasoning Merge

Fork the full reasoning environment, not just the text. Merges are validated for semantic coherence. Two chains of thought become one — without losing either.

The Vision

Not networking. Not citation.
Actual epistemic contact.

Two reasoning chains finding each other at the exact point of productive friction. Contribute precisely where your thinking has something to add. Collaboration at the speed of thought.

Get Involved
