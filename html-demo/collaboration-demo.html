The Merge Workspace — Reasoning
P1 When a collaborator joins, they don't just "open a file." They Fork the Environment. This gives them a private copy of the document and its entire background history. They can start their own chat-led exploration and test new hypotheses in a parallel branch — without affecting the master version.
P2 When a collaborator proposes an update, they don't just suggest new text; they propose a Reasoning Merge. The Reasoning Merge happens in a workspace where the Master Document and the Forked Branch are compared and analyzed side-by-side. In the bottom you find a chat interface — a portal to two distinct minds that you, the project owner, can interact with to determine the most meaningful synthesis.
P3 Behind the scenes, AI agents act as a proxy for two separate histories: Mind A (The Master) — the embodiment of the current document's memory, holding every piece of evidence and reasoning used to build the existing version. Mind B (The Branch) — holding the specific research, new logic, and breakthroughs that justify the proposed changes.
P4 When you open the Merge Workspace, an Assembler sits as a "Synthesis Layer" within the chat. You don't just talk to Mind A or Mind B; you ask the Assembler to map the terrain. "Assembler, where is the friction?" — it highlights the specific sentences where the two reasoning chains fundamentally disagree.
P5 This process transforms the merge into a high-fidelity exploration of the "Why." You can challenge new research, defend the original logic, or discover a third, superior path that neither side had seen in isolation. The merge only occurs once the reasoning "compiles" — meaning the new text and its underlying logic are fully reconciled and integrated into the master graph.
