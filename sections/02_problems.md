## Problem 2.1 — The Transition Tax

Your best thinking happens in conversation. It stays there.

Not because the thinking isn't worth sharing — but because moving it into a structured, shareable artifact costs more than most people are willing to pay. You'd have to stop, step out of the flow, rewrite everything from scratch in a different format, and lose half the nuance in the process. So you don't. The loop never closes. Thinking happens. Documenting happens. But never as the same act.

---

## Problem 2.2 — The Dimensionality Problem

You spent three hours thinking through a hard problem. A collaborator gets the last paragraph.

A document is a flat surface. You read it top to bottom — conclusions only, reasoning gone. Unlike a GitHub repo, where you navigate into the exact file, function, or commit relevant to you, a document gives you no entry point below the surface. You can comment on the text. You cannot enter the thought.

---

## Problem 2.3 — The Merge Problem

Two people work on the same problem in parallel. Both make real progress. Then they try to combine it.

There is no mechanism for this. You can merge text. You cannot merge reasoning. One chain of thought overwrites the other, or you end up with a compromise that captures neither. In code this problem is solved — pull request, diff, review, merge. In knowledge it has never existed.
